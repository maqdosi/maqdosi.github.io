Salesman

After highschool is when I landed my first real job. As a salesman in the TV/HiFi-department, I learnt how to creatively combine both the customers wishes with the demands of my employer. I thrived under the pressure of satisfying both, and realized my passion for sales. This turned out to be the beginning of my journey towards becomming a digital analyst.

VMware Hunter/Field Sales Executive

Having discovered my talent for sales at Media Markt, I was ready for the next step. Starting off by inviting customers across the Nordics to free VMware-webex's, I was quickly promoted to FSE. I realized however that my passion for sales stemmed from a passion for helping people. I decided to pursue this by applying to univeristy. Upon getting accepted to my bachelor in HR, my time at Tech Data had to come to an end.

Apple

While at university, I was headhunted for a retail sales position at the Apple Store in Täby. One of my goals with my HR-education was to one day use my retail experience from Media Markt and eventually landing a job as a HR-Business Partner at Apple one day. Hence, this was an opportunity I could not pass up, even though it might've been better to work part time within my field of study. Working for Apple had been, and was a dream for the most part. Organizational structure, team spirit and everyone pulling their shift taught me that everyone's got a role to play. Most importantly, I learnt that details really, really matter.

Recruiter/Employer Branding

Having realized my dream of working for Apple, it was time to combine my theoretical knowledge within HR with practical. I was headhunted by one of my future colleagues, and spent my last year in university working part time in IT-recruitment. Six months in, I was offered a full time job by the time I graduated. A few months after graduation, I was in charge of our Employer Branding. My experience in EB is what really turned my head away from HR, and ultimately to Digital Analytics. I realized that my passion far sales, and my passion for tech could be merged into a career. It took a while before I managed to take the leap and start pursuing my future as a Digital Analyst, but I have Webstep to thank for the experience I needed to realize that there was a better fit for me than HR.
